Ebbinghaus's Forgetting Curve

The Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve is a powerful concept that describes how memory retention declines over time. German psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus first developed this curve in the late 19th century through experiments where he memorized nonsensical syllables and recorded how much he retained over different time intervals. His findings revealed a steep initial drop in retention shortly after learning, followed by a gradual leveling off, illustrating that people forget information rapidly at first and then more slowly over time.

This phenomenon underscores the importance of timely reviews and spaced repetition to effectively consolidate information into long-term memory.
